# 3.1.5 Reading Level

**Level:** AAA
**Versions:** WCAG 2.0, 2.1, 2.2
**Principle:** 3 Understandable
**Guideline:** 3.1 Readable
**Reference:** [3.1.5 Reading Level](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/reading-level.html)

## Description

When text requires reading ability more advanced than the lower secondary education level after removal of proper names and titles, supplemental content, or a version that does not require reading ability more advanced than the lower secondary education level, is available.

## Sufficient Techniques

- [G86](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Techniques/general/G86): Providing a text summary that can be understood by people with lower secondary education level reading ability
- [G103](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Techniques/general/G103): Providing visual illustrations, pictures, and symbols to help explain ideas, events, and processes
- [G79](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Techniques/general/G79): Providing a spoken version of the text
- [G153](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Techniques/general/G153): Making the text easier to read
- [G160](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Techniques/general/G160): Providing sign language versions of information, ideas, and processes that must be understood in order to use the content
